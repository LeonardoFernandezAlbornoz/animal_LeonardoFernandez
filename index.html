THE EMPEROR PENGUIN

Features and characteristics

  • Scientific name: Aptenodytes forsteri
  • Genus: Aptenodytes
  • Family: Spheniscidae
  • Order: Sphenisciformes
  • Class: Aves
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Kingdom: Animalia

The emperor penguin, whose scientific name is Aptenodytes forsteri, belongs to the family Spheniscidae and is classified as a species
of sphenisciform bird, that is, it is part of the flightless seabirds that inhabit only the southern hemisphere.

Description of the emperor penguin

  1. Height: Between 120 and 140 centimeters
  2. Weight: around 40kg
  3. Habitat: Antartica

This species can reach a height of up to 1.22 meters, which makes it the largest and heaviest penguin, unlike the other penguins. Their
weight varies from 22 to 45 kg, the males being heavier.

The body of these birds, like other penguin species, is adapted to regulate their temperature to environmental conditions and their
hydrodynamic shape allows them to minimize water resistance when in contact with water. Their rigid, flat wings are as strong as flippers, allowing them to move easily through the Antarctic seas. They have 15 feathers per square centimeter, making them the birds with the largest amount of plumage.
